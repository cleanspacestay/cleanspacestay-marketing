Live Demo Walkthrough for Glenn

A screen-by-screen guide for the live platform demo. Keep it high-level, conversational, and anchored to what a CEO cares about: visibility, risk, and enterprise value.

Before You Start

Have These Open

Pull these up ahead of time so you can flip between them without fumbling:

  • Platform dashboard — logged in at app.cleanspacestay.com
  • A sample project with at least one active booking and one pending request
  • Your phone with the mobile app open (you'll hold it up or describe it — Glenn won't have it installed)
The Opening (30 Seconds)

Frame It as Enterprise Value, Not Travel

Glenn, what I want to show you isn't a travel tool. It's what happens when you take a process that lives in people's heads — texts, emails, spreadsheets — and put it into a system the company actually owns. Travel is just the first one we built. The playbook applies anywhere.
Tribal knowledge is key-person risk. Key-person risk holds the multiple down at exit. Owned systems with clean data lift it. That's the real story here.

Section 2 — 5–7 Minutes

The Platform Walkthrough

Stop 1: The Dashboard

This is the command center. Every active booking across every project is here. No digging through emails, no calling someone to ask where a crew is staying.
  • Project-based view — spend per job, per category
  • Active travelers and their status
  • Pending requests waiting for approval
This is the visibility you don't have today. Every dollar is tied to a project before it's spent, not after.

Stop 2: A Booking Request

Here's what a request looks like when it comes in. It's already tied to the project, the traveler, and the billing company. The person approving it has everything they need — no back-and-forth.
  • Project, traveler, dates, travel type — all captured at the point of request
  • Approval workflow (approve, deny, send back for changes)
  • Full audit trail — who requested, who approved, when
Every action is logged. If you ever need to answer "who approved this?" or "why did we spend this?" — it's right here.

Stop 3: Project-Level Spend

Pick any project. You can see exactly what's been spent on crew travel — broken down by hotel, flights, cars. In real time, not after the fact.
  • Total travel spend for the project
  • Breakdown by category
  • Budget tracking (if set)
You track labor to the hour. Now travel is tracked the same way. No more margin surprises at job close.

Stop 4: QuickBooks Reconciliation

This is where accounting lives. Charges are matched to bookings proactively — before month-end, not during the scramble.
  • Bookings matched to transactions
  • One-click reconciliation
  • No detective work — the system already knows which charge goes where
Your accounting team stops being reactive and starts being proactive. That's not just efficiency — that's margin protection.

Stop 5: Crew Map & Messaging

Real-time visibility. You can see where every crew is deployed right now — who's checked in, who's traveling, who's on-site. And you can message them directly from here.
  • Live map with crew locations
  • Check-in status (GPS-verified)
  • Direct and group messaging — no phone tag
No more calling around to find out where people are. The system tells you.

If Glenn Asks

Ready Answers

"What does this cost?"
It's not a subscription — it's owned software built for your operation. The investment is a fraction of what it saves, and unlike a subscription, it shows up as an asset on the balance sheet, not a recurring cost.
"Who built this?"
We worked with a development partner who specializes in operational platforms for specialty contractors. The code, the data, and the infrastructure are all ours.
"Can it do [other workflow]?"
That's exactly the conversation we want to have. The architecture is built to extend. Travel was first because it was the clearest pain point, but the same pattern applies to anything that currently lives in someone's head.
"How long did this take?"
We went from concept to working platform in a few months. The mobile app is live on iOS and Android. It's not a prototype — it's running in production today.
"What about Procore / other tools?"
This complements tools like Procore — it handles the operational workflows they don't cover. Crew travel, field coordination, real-time spend tracking by project. It fills the gap, it doesn't compete.
"What data do we get?"
Project-level travel spend, crew utilization, booking history, vendor costs, budget burn-down, reconciliation status — all exportable, all auditable, all tied to the job.
The One Thing to Remember

"The difference between a 3.5× company and a 7× company isn't revenue — it's how much of the operation runs on owned systems with clean data versus how much lives in people's heads. This is the first proof point. The playbook is repeatable."
