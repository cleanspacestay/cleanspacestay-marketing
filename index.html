The Problem

This Is How Most Companies Manage Crew Travel

If this sounds familiar, you're not alone — and you're losing money you don't even know about.

Scattered Everywhere

Booking confirmations in someone's inbox. Rates in a spreadsheet. Per diem on a sticky note. If your coordinator is out, nobody knows who's staying where.

Reactive Accounting

Charges hit the credit card. Then someone has to figure out which project it belongs to. Every month it's a scramble to reconcile against QuickBooks.

No Project-Level Visibility

You track labor costs to the hour — but crew travel? You don't know the real number until the job is done and the damage is already in your margins.

A $20M specialty contractor may spend $1M–$3M annually on crew travel. Most of it is unmanaged.
