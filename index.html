The Problem

This Is How Most Companies Manage Crew Travel

It works — until it doesn't. And none of it is visible, controllable, or transferable when it matters most.

Scattered Across Inboxes and Spreadsheets

Confirmations in email. Rates in Excel. Receipts in a folder somewhere. There's no single source of truth — so nobody has the full picture.

No Visibility by Project

You track labor to the hour — but crew travel stays invisible until the job closes. By then the margin hit is already baked in, with no data to prevent the next one.

Accounting Is Always Playing Catch-Up

Charges hit the card. Then someone scrambles to match them to a project. Month-end reconciliation in QuickBooks becomes a fire drill every single time.

It All Lives in One Person's Head

When the process lives with one person instead of in a system, it can't be audited, scaled, or handed off. That's not just a staffing risk — it's an operational capability the business doesn't actually own.

Across a multi-project operation, crew travel is one of the few major cost centers with no system of record. The bigger the operation, the more that blind spot costs — in dollars, in margin, and in risk no one can see.

How It Works

One Platform. Everyone on the Same Page.

The web platform and the mobile app are one system — a request made on a phone in the field flows through approval, booking, and reconciliation without anyone re-keying a thing.

1

The Crew Requests From the Field

A worker or foreman opens the app and requests travel — hotel, flight, rental car, or rideshare — for themselves or the whole crew. Tied to the project from the first tap.

2

The Right Person Approves From Their Phone

Foremen and project managers get a push notification and approve, deny, or send back for changes right on their phone. No texts, no phone tag, no email chains.

3

Travel Is Booked and Tracked

Every booking — confirmation number, dates, cost, receipt — lives under the project it belongs to. Crews check in on-site from the app, and the live map updates in real time.

4

Accounting Stays Ahead

Bookings are matched to QuickBooks transactions before the month-end scramble. Reconciliation is proactive, not reactive.
