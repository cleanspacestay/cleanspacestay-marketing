Executive Summary

A platform that pays for itself, then keeps paying.

Design-build and field-service firms track labor and materials to the penny, yet crew travel — flights, hotels, rideshares, per diems — often runs through forwarded email confirmations, personal credit cards, and a single coordinator's spreadsheet. It is real money, frequently in the seven figures annually, and it is the one major cost center with almost no structured visibility. When a job ends, no one can say cleanly what travel cost, which project it belonged to, or whether it was even necessary.

CleanSpace Stay closes that gap. It is a working platform and mobile-ready app that ingests booking confirmations automatically, ties every dollar to a specific project and crew member, routes approvals through the same Project Manager → Foreman → Worker structure these companies already run, and reconciles charges against accounting before they post. The result is not a dashboard for its own sake — it is recovered margin, eliminated administrative drag, and an auditable record of money that used to disappear into overhead.

The value of the platform is best understood through two lenses. The first is operational: what it saves the operating company every year. The second is strategic: what that saved margin, and the proprietary technology that produces it, is worth when the company is valued, sold, or rolled into a larger entity. The interactive model below quantifies both — and lets CleanSpace test it against its own real numbers.

How the value is derived

Two honest valuations, depending on how you buy it.

The arbitrage, in plain terms

A specialty contractor sells for roughly 3–5× EBITDA. So every $1 of margin this platform protects is not worth a dollar at exit — it is worth $4 or more, because a buyer pays a multiple on it. The savings the platform generates each year therefore translate into a far larger one-time lift in enterprise value the day the company sells. Owning the technology that produces those savings can raise the whole multiple on top of that.
